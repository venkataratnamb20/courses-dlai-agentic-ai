## Steps
Stages and Steps of a Systematic Literature Review:
1. Formulate Research Question(s) and Scope: Define a specific, answerable question (e.g., using PICO: Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome).
2. Develop a Review Protocol: Plan the methodology, including search strategies, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and assessment methods to reduce bias.
3. Conduct Literature Search: Search relevant databases using predefined keywords and search strings.
4. Screening and Selection: Screen titles, abstracts, and full texts based on the established inclusion/exclusion criteria.
5. Quality Assessment: Evaluate the quality and risk of bias for each included study.
6. Data Extraction and Management: Systematically extract relevant information from the studies.
7. Data Synthesis and Analysis: Analyze and combine the data (qualitatively or quantitatively) to answer the research question.
8. Interpretation and Reporting: Present findings, discuss limitations, and publish the results, often following PRISMA guidelines.

**Common Frameworks:**
- `SALSA:` Search, Appraisal, Synthesis, Analysis.
- `PSALSAR:` Protocol, Search, Appraisal, Synthesis, Analysis, Report.
- `PIECES:` Planning, Identifying, Evaluating, Collecting/Combining, Explaining, Summarizing.
